In Gideon v. Wainwright, 372 U.S. 335 (1963), the Supreme Court held that the Sixth Amendment right to counsel is a fundamental right made obligatory on the states by the Fourteenth Amendment, so that an indigent defendant in a state criminal prosecution must be provided with appointed counsel. (Gideon v. Wainwright, 372 U.S. 335 (1963) (Black, J.))
